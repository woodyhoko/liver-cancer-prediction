🧬 Transcriptomics · TCGA-LIHC · Survival

Does tumour mRNA
predict survival?

An exploratory look at the public TCGA Liver Hepatocellular Carcinoma cohort: split patients by survival outcome, then scan tumour gene-expression (mRNA) to find the genes whose levels differ between those who survived and those who did not.

▶ Explore the Analysis 📖 Documentation ⌥ Source

Built only on publicly released, de-identified TCGA data — not a clinical tool, and no private patient details are shared here.

the cohort
Public TCGA-LIHC, framed as one question.
~377
patients with clinical + RNA-Seq data
2 groups
survived vs. deceased, by survival status
≈70%
accuracy: 5-yr survival above ~50% or not
approach
A simple per-gene survival screen.
🧬

mRNA expression

Median RNA-Seq expression per gene, one column per tumour sample, matched to each patient by TCGA ID.

🪓

Split by outcome

Patients are grouped by Overall Survival Status — living vs. deceased — to compare the two populations.

📈

Per-gene z-test

For each gene, a two-sample z-test (statsmodels) flags whether expression differs between the survival groups.

⚠️

Exploratory only

No multiple-testing correction, one modest cohort, binary survival framing — directional signal, not a validated predictor.
